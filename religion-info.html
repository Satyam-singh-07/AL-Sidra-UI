The Five Pillars of Islam

The five pillars of Islam are the foundation of Muslim life:

  1. Shahadah: Declaration of faith
  2. Salah: Prayer five times daily
  3. Zakat: Giving to charity
  4. Sawm: Fasting during Ramadan
  5. Hajj: Pilgrimage to Mecca

Prayer
How to Perform Wudu (Ablution)

Wudu is the Islamic procedure for washing parts of the body before prayer:

  • Make intention (Niyyah)
  • Wash hands three times
  • Rinse mouth three times
  • Clean nose three times
  • Wash face three times
  • Wash arms up to elbows three times
  • Wipe head once
  • Wash feet up to ankles three times

Fasting
Rules of Ramadan Fasting

Who must fast? All adult Muslims who are physically and mentally capable.

Exemptions: Children, elderly, pregnant/nursing women, travelers, and those who are ill.

Timing: From dawn (Fajr) to sunset (Maghrib).

Invalidators: Eating, drinking intentionally, marital relations during fasting hours.

Charity
Zakat Calculation Guide

Nisab: Minimum amount of wealth one must have before Zakat is due.

Current Nisab: 87.48 grams of gold or 612.36 grams of silver.

Rate: 2.5% of total savings and wealth above Nisab.

When to pay: After one lunar year of holding the wealth.

Recipients: Eight categories mentioned in Quran (9:60).

The Five Pillars of Islam

The five pillars of Islam are the foundation of Muslim life:

  1. Shahadah: Declaration of faith - "There is no god but Allah, and Muhammad is His messenger."
  2. Salah: Prayer five times daily facing Mecca
  3. Zakat: Giving to charity - typically 2.5% of savings annually
  4. Sawm: Fasting during the month of Ramadan from dawn to sunset
  5. Hajj: Pilgrimage to Mecca at least once in a lifetime if physically and financially able

These pillars form the foundation of Muslim life and are considered obligatory acts of worship.
